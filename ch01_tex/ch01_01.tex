\section*{Problem 1}

\textbf{Problem:} Prove by vector methods that the diagonals of a rhombus are orthogonal.

\bigskip
\textbf{Solution:}

A rhombus is a parallelogram with all four sides of equal length. Let the two adjacent sides of the rhombus be represented by vectors $\vect{a}$ and $\vect{b}$, where $|\vect{a}| = |\vect{b}|$ since all sides are equal.

The two diagonals of the rhombus are:
\begin{align}
\vect{d}_1 &= \vect{a} + \vect{b} \quad \text{(one diagonal)}, \\
\vect{d}_2 &= \vect{a} - \vect{b} \quad \text{(the other diagonal)}.
\end{align}

To show the diagonals are orthogonal, we compute their scalar (dot) product:
\begin{align}
\vect{d}_1 \cdot \vect{d}_2 &= (\vect{a} + \vect{b}) \cdot (\vect{a} - \vect{b}).
\end{align}

Expanding this using the distributive property of the dot product:
\begin{align}
\vect{d}_1 \cdot \vect{d}_2 &= \vect{a} \cdot \vect{a} - \vect{a} \cdot \vect{b} + \vect{b} \cdot \vect{a} - \vect{b} \cdot \vect{b} \\
&= |\vect{a}|^2 - |\vect{b}|^2.
\end{align}

Since the rhombus has all sides equal, $|\vect{a}| = |\vect{b}|$, so:
\begin{equation}
\vect{d}_1 \cdot \vect{d}_2 = |\vect{a}|^2 - |\vect{a}|^2 = 0.
\end{equation}

Since $\vect{d}_1 \cdot \vect{d}_2 = 0$, the diagonals are perpendicular. \hfill $\blacksquare$

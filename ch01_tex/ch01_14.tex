\section*{Problem 14}

\textbf{Problem:} Show that the trace of the electromagnetic energy-momentum tensor $T_{\mu\nu}$ is zero, where
\[
T_{\mu\nu} = \frac{1}{4\pi}\!\left(F_{\mu\lambda}F_{\nu\lambda} - \tfrac{1}{4}\,\delta_{\mu\nu}\,F_{\lambda\sigma}F_{\lambda\sigma}\right),
\]
and $F_{\mu\nu}$ is the electromagnetic field tensor of Problem 13.

\bigskip
\textbf{Solution:}

The trace of $T_{\mu\nu}$ is obtained by contracting $\mu$ with $\nu$ (setting $\mu = \nu$ and summing):
\begin{align}
T_{\mu\mu} &= \frac{1}{4\pi}\!\left(F_{\mu\lambda}F_{\mu\lambda} - \tfrac{1}{4}\,\delta_{\mu\mu}\,F_{\lambda\sigma}F_{\lambda\sigma}\right).
\end{align}

Now evaluate each piece:

\textbf{First term:} $F_{\mu\lambda}F_{\mu\lambda}$ is a sum over both $\mu$ and $\lambda$---this is just $F_{\lambda\sigma}F_{\lambda\sigma}$ (with dummy indices renamed):
\[
F_{\mu\lambda}F_{\mu\lambda} = F_{\lambda\sigma}F_{\lambda\sigma}.
\]

\textbf{Second term:} $\delta_{\mu\mu}$ is the trace of the identity matrix in four dimensions:
\[
\delta_{\mu\mu} = 4.
\]

Substituting:
\begin{align}
T_{\mu\mu} &= \frac{1}{4\pi}\!\left(F_{\lambda\sigma}F_{\lambda\sigma} - \tfrac{1}{4}\cdot 4 \cdot F_{\lambda\sigma}F_{\lambda\sigma}\right) \\
&= \frac{1}{4\pi}\!\left(F_{\lambda\sigma}F_{\lambda\sigma} - F_{\lambda\sigma}F_{\lambda\sigma}\right) \\
&= \boxed{0}.
\end{align}

The trace of the electromagnetic energy-momentum tensor vanishes. \hfill $\blacksquare$

\bigskip
\textit{Physical significance:} The vanishing trace $T_{\mu\mu} = 0$ is a consequence of the conformal invariance of the electromagnetic field in four dimensions. In terms of the physical fields $\vect{E}$ and $\vect{B}$, one can verify that:
\[
T_{\mu\mu} \propto |\vect{E}|^2 - |\vect{B}|^2 - (|\vect{E}|^2 - |\vect{B}|^2) = 0.
\]

More specifically, the diagonal components are:
\begin{align}
T_{44} &= \frac{1}{8\pi}(|\vect{E}|^2 + |\vect{B}|^2) \quad \text{(energy density)}, \\
T_{ii} &= \text{(Maxwell stress tensor components)},
\end{align}
and their sum (the trace in 4D) vanishes identically. This reflects the fact that the photon is massless---a massive field would have $T_{\mu\mu} \neq 0$.

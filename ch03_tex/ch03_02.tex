\section*{Chapter 3, Problem 2}

\textbf{Problem.} Show that three vectors in three-dimensional space are linearly dependent if and only if $\mathbf{A} \cdot (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}) = 0$.

\bigskip
\textbf{Solution.}

Let $\mathbf{A}$, $\mathbf{B}$, $\mathbf{C}$ be three vectors in $\mathbb{R}^3$.

\medskip
\textbf{($\Rightarrow$) If $\mathbf{A}$, $\mathbf{B}$, $\mathbf{C}$ are linearly dependent, then $\mathbf{A}\cdot(\mathbf{B}\times\mathbf{C})=0$:}

If the three vectors are linearly dependent, then one of them can be written as a linear combination of the other two. Without loss of generality, suppose
\[
\mathbf{A} = \alpha \mathbf{B} + \beta \mathbf{C}
\]
for some scalars $\alpha, \beta$. Then
\[
\mathbf{A}\cdot(\mathbf{B}\times\mathbf{C}) = (\alpha \mathbf{B} + \beta \mathbf{C})\cdot(\mathbf{B}\times\mathbf{C}) = \alpha\,\mathbf{B}\cdot(\mathbf{B}\times\mathbf{C}) + \beta\,\mathbf{C}\cdot(\mathbf{B}\times\mathbf{C}).
\]
Now $\mathbf{B}\cdot(\mathbf{B}\times\mathbf{C})=0$ because $\mathbf{B}\times\mathbf{C}$ is perpendicular to $\mathbf{B}$, and similarly $\mathbf{C}\cdot(\mathbf{B}\times\mathbf{C})=0$ because $\mathbf{B}\times\mathbf{C}$ is perpendicular to $\mathbf{C}$. Therefore $\mathbf{A}\cdot(\mathbf{B}\times\mathbf{C})=0$.

\medskip
\textbf{($\Leftarrow$) If $\mathbf{A}\cdot(\mathbf{B}\times\mathbf{C})=0$, then $\mathbf{A}$, $\mathbf{B}$, $\mathbf{C}$ are linearly dependent:}

Write the vectors in component form:
$\mathbf{A} = (A_1, A_2, A_3)$, $\mathbf{B} = (B_1, B_2, B_3)$, $\mathbf{C} = (C_1, C_2, C_3)$.

The scalar triple product equals the determinant:
\[
\mathbf{A}\cdot(\mathbf{B}\times\mathbf{C}) = \det\begin{pmatrix} A_1 & A_2 & A_3 \\ B_1 & B_2 & B_3 \\ C_1 & C_2 & C_3 \end{pmatrix}.
\]
If this determinant is zero, then the matrix formed by the three vectors as rows is singular. By a fundamental theorem of linear algebra, the rows of a singular matrix are linearly dependent. Therefore $\mathbf{A}$, $\mathbf{B}$, $\mathbf{C}$ are linearly dependent.

\medskip
Combining both directions, three vectors in $\mathbb{R}^3$ are linearly dependent if and only if $\mathbf{A}\cdot(\mathbf{B}\times\mathbf{C})=0$. \hfill $\blacksquare$

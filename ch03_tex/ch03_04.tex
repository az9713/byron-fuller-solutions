\section*{Chapter 3, Problem 4}

\textbf{Problem.} Prove that any set of $n + 1$ vectors in an $n$-dimensional vector space is linearly dependent.

\bigskip
\textbf{Solution.}

Let $V$ be an $n$-dimensional vector space with basis $\{\mathbf{e}_1, \mathbf{e}_2, \ldots, \mathbf{e}_n\}$. Consider $n+1$ vectors $\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2, \ldots, \mathbf{v}_{n+1} \in V$. Each can be expressed in terms of the basis:
\[
\mathbf{v}_j = \sum_{i=1}^{n} a_{ij}\, \mathbf{e}_i, \quad j = 1, 2, \ldots, n+1.
\]

We wish to show there exist scalars $c_1, c_2, \ldots, c_{n+1}$, not all zero, such that
\[
\sum_{j=1}^{n+1} c_j \mathbf{v}_j = \mathbf{0}.
\]
Substituting the basis expansions:
\[
\sum_{j=1}^{n+1} c_j \sum_{i=1}^{n} a_{ij}\, \mathbf{e}_i = \sum_{i=1}^{n}\left(\sum_{j=1}^{n+1} a_{ij}\, c_j\right) \mathbf{e}_i = \mathbf{0}.
\]

Since the $\mathbf{e}_i$ are linearly independent, each coefficient must vanish:
\[
\sum_{j=1}^{n+1} a_{ij}\, c_j = 0, \quad i = 1, 2, \ldots, n.
\]

This is a homogeneous system of $n$ linear equations in $n+1$ unknowns $c_1, c_2, \ldots, c_{n+1}$. A homogeneous system with more unknowns than equations always has a nontrivial solution (since the rank of the coefficient matrix is at most $n$, leaving at least one free variable).

Therefore there exist $c_1, \ldots, c_{n+1}$, not all zero, such that $\sum_{j=1}^{n+1} c_j \mathbf{v}_j = \mathbf{0}$, which means the $n+1$ vectors are linearly dependent. \hfill $\blacksquare$

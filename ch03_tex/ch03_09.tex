\section*{Chapter 3, Problem 9}

\textbf{Problem.} Define the commutator $[A, B]$ of two matrices by $[A, B] = AB - BA$, and the anticommutator $\{A, B\}$ by $\{A, B\} = AB + BA$. Show that
\begin{align*}
&\text{(a)}\quad [A, B] = -[B, A], \quad \{A, B\} = \{B, A\}, \\
&\text{(b)}\quad [A, B, C] = [A, B]C + B[A, C], \\
&\text{(c)}\quad [A, [B, C]] + [B, [C, A]] + [C, [A, B]] = 0.
\end{align*}

\bigskip
\textbf{Solution.}

\textbf{(a)} Antisymmetry of commutator:
\[
[A, B] = AB - BA = -(BA - AB) = -[B, A]. \quad \checkmark
\]
Symmetry of anticommutator:
\[
\{A, B\} = AB + BA = BA + AB = \{B, A\}. \quad \checkmark
\]

\medskip
\textbf{(b)} This is the ``Leibniz rule'' for commutators. We compute:
\[
[A, BC] = A(BC) - (BC)A = ABC - BCA.
\]
On the other hand:
\[
[A, B]C + B[A, C] = (AB - BA)C + B(AC - CA) = ABC - BAC + BAC - BCA = ABC - BCA.
\]
These are equal, so $[A, BC] = [A, B]C + B[A, C]$. \hfill $\checkmark$

\medskip
\textbf{(c)} Jacobi identity. We expand each term:
\begin{align*}
[A, [B, C]] &= A(BC - CB) - (BC - CB)A = ABC - ACB - BCA + CBA, \\
[B, [C, A]] &= B(CA - AC) - (CA - AC)B = BCA - BAC - CAB + ACB, \\
[C, [A, B]] &= C(AB - BA) - (AB - BA)C = CAB - CBA - ABC + BAC.
\end{align*}
Adding all three:
\begin{align*}
&(ABC - ACB - BCA + CBA) \\
+\; &(BCA - BAC - CAB + ACB) \\
+\; &(CAB - CBA - ABC + BAC) \\
= \; &(ABC - ABC) + (-ACB + ACB) + (-BCA + BCA) \\
&+ (CBA - CBA) + (-BAC + BAC) + (-CAB + CAB) = 0.
\end{align*}
\hfill $\blacksquare$

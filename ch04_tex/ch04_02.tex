{\Large\bfseries Chapter 4, Problem 2}\\[6pt]
Byron \& Fuller, \textit{Mathematics of Classical and Quantum Physics}

\bigskip

\textbf{Problem.} Prove that if the matrices $A$ and $B$ are related by a similarity transformation, $A = P^{-1}BP$, and $P$ is unitary, then the similarity transformation preserves the adjoint.

\bigskip
\textbf{Solution.}

We are given $A = P^{-1}BP$ where $P$ is unitary, so $P^{-1} = P^\dagger$.

We need to show that if $A = P^\dagger B P$, then $A^\dagger = P^\dagger B^\dagger P$. That is, the adjoint of $A$ is related to the adjoint of $B$ by the same similarity transformation.

Compute $A^\dagger$:
\[
A^\dagger = (P^\dagger B P)^\dagger.
\]
Using the property $(XYZ)^\dagger = Z^\dagger Y^\dagger X^\dagger$:
\[
A^\dagger = P^\dagger B^\dagger (P^\dagger)^\dagger = P^\dagger B^\dagger P.
\]
Since $(P^\dagger)^\dagger = P$.

Therefore:
\[
A^\dagger = P^\dagger B^\dagger P = P^{-1} B^\dagger P,
\]
which shows that $A^\dagger$ is related to $B^\dagger$ by the same unitary similarity transformation. The adjoint is preserved under unitary similarity transformations. \qed

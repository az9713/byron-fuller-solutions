\section*{Problem 5.30}

\textbf{Problem:} Verify that for all $z$ not in the spectrum of $A$,
\[
(A - zI)^{-1} = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\frac{1}{\lambda - z}\,dE_\lambda,
\]
where $A$ is a self-adjoint operator whose spectral resolution is $E_\lambda(A)$.

\bigskip
\textbf{Solution:}

By the spectral theorem, the self-adjoint operator $A$ has the representation:
\[
A = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\lambda\,dE_\lambda.
\]

For $z \notin \sigma(A)$ (the spectrum of $A$), the function $g(\lambda) = 1/(\lambda - z)$ is bounded and continuous on the spectrum. By the functional calculus for self-adjoint operators, we can define:
\[
g(A) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}g(\lambda)\,dE_\lambda = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\frac{1}{\lambda - z}\,dE_\lambda.
\]

We need to show that $g(A) = (A - zI)^{-1}$, i.e., $(A - zI)\,g(A) = I$.

\subsection*{Verification}

Compute $(A - zI)\,g(A)$ using the multiplicative property of the spectral calculus:
\[
(A - zI)\,g(A) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}(\lambda - z)\,dE_\lambda \cdot \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\frac{1}{\lambda' - z}\,dE_{\lambda'}.
\]

By the product rule for spectral integrals:
\[
(A - zI)\,g(A) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}(\lambda - z)\cdot\frac{1}{\lambda - z}\,dE_\lambda = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}1\,dE_\lambda = E_\infty - E_{-\infty} = I.
\]

Similarly:
\[
g(A)(A - zI) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\frac{1}{\lambda-z}\cdot(\lambda-z)\,dE_\lambda = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}dE_\lambda = I.
\]

Therefore $g(A)$ is both a left and right inverse of $(A - zI)$:
\[
\boxed{(A - zI)^{-1} = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\frac{1}{\lambda - z}\,dE_\lambda.}
\]

This operator is called the \textbf{resolvent} of $A$ at $z$, often denoted $R_z(A)$. It exists precisely when $z$ is not in the spectrum of $A$, since $1/(\lambda - z)$ must be bounded on the spectrum.

The key property used is that the spectral calculus is a homomorphism: $f(A)g(A) = (fg)(A)$, and $(\lambda - z)\cdot\frac{1}{\lambda - z} = 1$ for all $\lambda$ in the spectrum when $z\notin\sigma(A)$.

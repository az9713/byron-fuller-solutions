\section*{Chapter 7, Problem 9}

\textbf{Problem:} Using the results of the previous problem, find the Green's function for the general third-order linear differential operator,
\[
\frac{d^3u}{dx^3} + a_1\frac{d^2u}{dx^2} + a_2\frac{du}{dx} + a_3\,u = f(x),
\]
in terms of the roots $r_1, r_2$, and $r_3$ of the characteristic polynomial
\[
r^3 + a_1 r^2 + a_2 r + a_3 = 0.
\]

Assume the Green's function and its first two derivatives vanish at $x = 0$.

\bigskip
\textbf{Solution:}

For constant coefficients, the three linearly independent solutions of the homogeneous equation are:
\[
u_k(x) = e^{r_k x}, \quad k = 1, 2, 3,
\]
where $r_1, r_2, r_3$ are the roots of the characteristic polynomial (assumed distinct).

The Wronskian is:
\[
W(x) = \begin{vmatrix} e^{r_1 x} & e^{r_2 x} & e^{r_3 x} \\ r_1 e^{r_1 x} & r_2 e^{r_2 x} & r_3 e^{r_3 x} \\ r_1^2 e^{r_1 x} & r_2^2 e^{r_2 x} & r_3^2 e^{r_3 x} \end{vmatrix}.
\]

Factoring out the exponentials:
\[
W(x) = e^{(r_1+r_2+r_3)x}\begin{vmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ r_1 & r_2 & r_3 \\ r_1^2 & r_2^2 & r_3^2 \end{vmatrix}.
\]

The remaining determinant is the Vandermonde determinant:
\[
V = (r_2 - r_1)(r_3 - r_1)(r_3 - r_2).
\]

Since $f_0 = 1$ and $f_1 = a_1$, by Abel's identity: $W(x) = W(0)e^{-a_1 x}$. Also, $r_1 + r_2 + r_3 = -a_1$, confirming consistency.

From Problem 8, the Green's function with the boundary condition $G(0,x') = G'(0,x') = G''(0,x') = 0$ (causal Green's function) is:
\[
G(x,x') = \begin{cases} 0, & x < x', \\ \displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{3} \frac{e^{r_k x}\,\Delta_k(x')}{W(x')}, & x > x'. \end{cases}
\]

The cofactors $\Delta_k(x')$ are obtained from the last row of the Wronskian. Specifically:
\begin{align}
\Delta_1(x') &= (-1)^{3+1}\begin{vmatrix} e^{r_2 x'} & e^{r_3 x'} \\ r_2 e^{r_2 x'} & r_3 e^{r_3 x'} \end{vmatrix} = (r_3 - r_2)e^{(r_2+r_3)x'}, \\
\Delta_2(x') &= (-1)^{3+2}\begin{vmatrix} e^{r_1 x'} & e^{r_3 x'} \\ r_1 e^{r_1 x'} & r_3 e^{r_3 x'} \end{vmatrix} = -(r_3 - r_1)e^{(r_1+r_3)x'}, \\
\Delta_3(x') &= (-1)^{3+3}\begin{vmatrix} e^{r_1 x'} & e^{r_2 x'} \\ r_1 e^{r_1 x'} & r_2 e^{r_2 x'} \end{vmatrix} = (r_2 - r_1)e^{(r_1+r_2)x'}.
\end{align}

Therefore, for $x > x'$:
\begin{align}
G(x,x') &= \frac{1}{V\,e^{(r_1+r_2+r_3)x'}}\Bigl[(r_3-r_2)e^{r_1 x+(r_2+r_3)x'} \\
&\qquad -(r_3-r_1)e^{r_2 x+(r_1+r_3)x'} + (r_2-r_1)e^{r_3 x+(r_1+r_2)x'}\Bigr] \\
&= \frac{1}{V}\Bigl[(r_3-r_2)e^{r_1(x-x')} - (r_3-r_1)e^{r_2(x-x')} + (r_2-r_1)e^{r_3(x-x')}\Bigr].
\end{align}

Writing $\tau = x - x'$ and $V = (r_2-r_1)(r_3-r_1)(r_3-r_2)$:
\[
\boxed{G(x,x') = \theta(x-x')\sum_{k=1}^{3}\frac{e^{r_k(x-x')}}{\prod_{j\neq k}(r_k - r_j)}}
\]
where $\theta$ is the Heaviside step function.

One can verify: $G(x',x') = 0$, $G'(x',x') = 0$ (from the partial fraction identity $\sum_k 1/\prod_{j\neq k}(r_k-r_j) = 0$ and $\sum_k r_k/\prod_{j\neq k}(r_k-r_j) = 0$), and $G''(x'^+,x') = 1$ (from $\sum_k r_k^2/\prod_{j\neq k}(r_k-r_j) = 1$). $\blacksquare$

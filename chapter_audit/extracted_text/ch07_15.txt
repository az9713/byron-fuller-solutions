Relation to Section 7.2: In Section 7.2, the Green’s function is constructed by finding
two solutions that satisfy the boundary conditions at each endpoint and matching them at
x = x′. Here, e−κx decays as x → +∞ and e+κx decays as x → −∞ . The Green’s function
is their product (divided by the Wronskian), which is exactly what the Fourier method
produces automatically.
For the scattering case ( E > 0), the poles p = ±k lie on the real axis, and the Fourier
integral is ambiguous — one must specify an iϵ prescription (outgoing vs. incoming waves),
corresponding to a genuine physical boundary-condition choice. The fact that no such choice
was needed for E < 0 is because the decay boundary condition is the only physical option,
and it is automatically built into the requirement of Fourier transformability. ■

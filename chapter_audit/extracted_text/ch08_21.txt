Chapter 8, Problem 21. At the beginning of Section 8.6 in discussing kernels of finite
rank, we introduced briefly the notion of a linear functional. The purpose of this problem
and the two that follow is to introduce some of the simpler aspects of linear functionals on
a Banach space. We make the following definition: A functional F on a Banach space B is
an operation which assigns to every g ∈ B a complex number (assuming that B is defined
over the field of complex numbers). The action of F on g is denoted by F (g). F is said
to be linear if F (αg1 + βg2) = αF (g1) + βF (g2) for all complex numbers α and β, and all
g1, g2 ∈ B. F is said to be bounded if there exists a real number M such that |F (g)| ≤ M ∥g∥
for all g ∈ B, M being independent of g.

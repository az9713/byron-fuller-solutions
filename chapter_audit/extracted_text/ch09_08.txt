Problem. (a) Show that ψ′ = at(1 − t)(1 − 2t) is orthogonal to sin πt on [0, 1], making it
an appropriate trial function for the second largest eigenvalue of the operator A with kernel
a(t, t′) = t<(1 − t>).
(b) Using ψ′, estimate the second largest eigenvalue and compare with the exact value
λ2 = 1/(4π2).
Solution.
(a) Orthogonality to sin πt
Using the operator P from Problem 9.6: P f(t) = f (1 − t).
The function sin πt is symmetric under P : sin π(1 − t) = sin πt (eigenvalue +1).
The function ψ′(t) = at(1 − t)(1 − 2t) is antisymmetric under P :
ψ′(1 − t) = a(1 − t) · t · (1 − 2(1 − t)) = a(1 − t) · t · (2t − 1) = −at(1 − t)(1 − 2t) = −ψ′(t).
So P ψ′ = −ψ′ (eigenvalue −1).
Since P is self-adjoint and sin πt and ψ′ belong to different eigenvalues of P , they are
automatically orthogonal:
(ψ′, sin πt) = 0 .
This can also be verified by direct integration, but the symmetry argument is much
simpler. Since sin πt is the eigenvector of A with the largest eigenvalue, orthogonality to it
means ψ′ is appropriate for estimating the second eigenvalue via the variational method. □

Byron & Fuller, Mathematics of Classical and Quantum Physics
Problem. Consider the hydrogen atom with Hamiltonian H0 and eigenstates ψnℓm. The
2s–2p states are nearly degenerate (split by the Lamb shift L ≈ 4 × 10−6 eV). An electric
field H1 = eEz is applied.
(a) Explain why we can restrict attention to the 2s–2p subspace.
(b) Show that the Brillouin–Wigner perturbation series (Eq. 9.34) has nonvanishing first
two terms for ψ2s in terms of ψ2p0, while the Rayleigh–Schrödinger series (Eq. 9.40) would
not.
(c) Find ψ2s and λ2s in terms of L and M = ⟨ψ2s, H1ψ2p0⟩.
(d) Take the limit L → 0.
Solution.
(a) Restriction to the 2s–2p subspace
The perturbation H1 = eEz shifts energies by amounts of order M ∼ eEa0 ∼ L (where a0
is the Bohr radius). The Lamb shift L ≈ 4 × 10−6 eV separates the 2s and 2p levels.
The nearest states outside the 2s–2p manifold are the 3s, 3p, 3d states, which lie about
∆E ≈ 2 eV away. Since L ≪ ∆E, the mixing with these distant states is of order M/∆E ≪ 1
and can be neglected. The perturbation theory of Section 9.2 (for nearly degenerate states)
is specifically designed for this situation: states that are nearly degenerate must be treated
non-perturbatively among themselves, while far-away states contribute negligible corrections.
Furthermore, by the selection rules for the matrix elements of z = r cos θ, H1 only
connects states whose ℓ values differ by 1, so within the 2s–2p manifold, only ⟨2s|H1|2p0⟩ =
M is nonzero (the 2p± states have ∆m = ±1 and don’t couple to 2s via z).

Fig. 9.4 shows both the exact result and the first iterative (Born) approximation. For the
exact result, δ0 extracted from Re and Im parts must be consistent (same δ0 from either). For

Uniqueness of δ0
The phase shift δ0 is determined only modulo π from the scattering amplitude, since:
eiδ0 sin δ0 = ei(δ0+nπ) sin(δ0 + nπ) for integer n.
Wait—actually sin(δ0 + π) = − sin δ0 and ei(δ0+π) = −eiδ0, so the product is unchanged.
Therefore δ0 is determined modulo π.
By convention, δ0 is chosen to be a continuous function of q1 with δ0 → 0 as q1 → ∞ (the
Levinson theorem may add nπ at q1 = 0 depending on the number of bound states). For a
repulsive Yukawa potential with no bound states, δ0(0) = 0 and δ0 remains in (−π/2, 0) for
all q1.
δ0 is determined modulo π; the physical choice is fixed by continuity and δ0(∞) = 0 .

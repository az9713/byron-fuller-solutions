(c) Numerical implementation (outline)
For the attractive Yukawa potential ( mV0/p2ℏ2 = −1.4), one would:
1. Discretize [0, rmax] into N points (choosing rmax large enough that V (rmax) ≈ 0).
2. Construct the N × N kernel matrix from Eqs. (9.112)–(9.113).
3. Solve (I − λhK)f = g by matrix inversion.
4. Extract F0(p1) and δ0(p1) for a range of momenta p1.
The attractive potential supports an s-wave bound state, which manifests as:
• δ0(0) = π (by Levinson’s theorem, one bound state adds π to δ0(0)).
• A dramatic change in the behavior of δ0(p1) compared to the repulsive case.
• The scattering amplitude shows enhanced low-energy scattering.

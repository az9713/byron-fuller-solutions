Chapter 10, Problem 3
Problem. Find the symmetry group, including only rotations, of a square, rectangular
parallelepiped (i.e., square on the top and bottom and rectangular on the sides). Show that
this group is isomorphic to the symmetry group of the square which includes both rotations
and reflection. What are the classes? Show that there is an invariant subgroup. Find the
factor group associated with this invariant subgroup.
Solution.
Symmetry group of the square parallelepiped (rotations only):
Consider a rectangular parallelepiped with a square cross-section (top and bottom are
squares, sides are rectangles). The pure rotation symmetries are:
1. e: identity
2. C4: rotation by 90◦ about the vertical axis (through centers of square faces)
3. C 2
4 = C2: rotation by 180◦ about the vertical axis
4. C 3
4 : rotation by 270◦ about the vertical axis
5. C ′
2: rotation by 180◦ about a horizontal axis through midpoints of opposite square
edges (say the x-axis)
6. C ′′
2 : rotation by 180◦ about the other horizontal axis through midpoints of opposite
square edges (the y-axis)
7. C (d)
2 : rotation by 180◦ about one diagonal axis of the square face
8. C (d′)
2 : rotation by 180◦ about the other diagonal axis of the square face
This is a group of order 8, called D4 (the dihedral group of order 8).

Chapter 10, Problem 4
Problem. Determine all the elements of the group of rotational symmetries of the regular
tetrahedron (i.e., a tetrahedron whose sides are equilateral triangles). Put these elements
into the classes to which they belong. Find all the subgroups. Are there any invariant
subgroups?
Solution.
Label the four vertices of the regular tetrahedron as 1, 2, 3, 4. The rotation group
of the regular tetrahedron is isomorphic to the alternating group A4 (the group of even
permutations of four objects), and has order 12.
The 12 elements:
1. Identity (1 element): e
2. Rotations by 120◦ and 240◦ about axes through each vertex and the center
of the opposite face (8 elements): There are 4 such axes, each giving two nontrivial
rotations.
Axis through vertex 1: (234), (243)
Axis through vertex 2: (134), (143)
Axis through vertex 3: (124), (142)
Axis through vertex 4: (123), (132)
3. Rotations by 180◦ about axes through midpoints of opposite edges (3 ele-
ments):
Axis through edges 12 and 34: (12)(34)
Axis through edges 13 and 24: (13)(24)
Axis through edges 14 and 23: (14)(23)
Total: 1 + 8 + 3 = 12 elements. ✓
Conjugacy Classes:
Elements are conjugate if and only if they have the same cycle structure in A4:
C1 = {e} (1 element)
C2 = {(123), (132), (124), (142), (134), (143), (234), (243)} (8 elements)
Wait—in A4 the 3-cycles actually split into two classes. Let us verify. In S4, all 3-cycles
are conjugate, but in A4 they can split. We check: (12)(34) · (123) · (12)(34) = (243) . Since
(12)(34) ∈ A4, we get (123) ∼ (243). Also (123)2 = (132), and conjugating (123) by (13)(24)
gives (142).
1

In fact in A4, the 3-cycles split into two conjugacy classes of 4 elements each:
C2 = {(123), (142), (134), (243)} (4 elements)
C3 = {(132), (143), (124), (234)} (4 elements)
C4 = {(12)(34), (13)(24), (14)(23)} (3 elements)
Check: 1 + 4 + 4 + 3 = 12 . ✓
So there are 4 conjugacy classes .
Subgroups:
By Lagrange’s theorem, subgroup orders must divide 12, so possible orders are 1, 2, 3,
4, 6, 12.
• Order 1: {e}
• Order 2: {e, (12)(34)}, {e, (13)(24)}, {e, (14)(23)} — three subgroups.
• Order 3: {e, (123), (132)}, {e, (124), (142)}, {e, (134), (143)}, {e, (234), (243)} — four
subgroups (cyclic, generated by 3-cycles).
• Order 4: V = {e, (12)(34), (13)(24), (14)(23)} — the Klein four-group. This is the
only subgroup of order 4.
• Order 6: There is no subgroup of order 6 in A4. (If there were, it would be normal
of index 2, but one can verify no such subgroup exists.)
• Order 12: A4 itself.
Invariant (Normal) Subgroups:
A normal subgroup must be a union of conjugacy classes. The Klein four-group V =
{e, (12)(34), (13)(24), (14)(23)} is the union of C1 and C4, with |V | = 1 + 3 = 4, which divides
12. One can verify it is closed under multiplication. Therefore V is a normal subgroup.
No other nontrivial proper normal subgroup exists in A4. So the only invariant sub-
group is the Klein four-group V ∼= Z2 × Z2.
The factor group A4/V has order 12/4 = 3 and is therefore isomorphic to Z3.

Chapter 10, Problem 22
Problem. In quantum mechanics one defines an electron spin wave function using α for spin
“up” and β for spin “down”; α and β are orthonormal. For a system of three electrons we
can form product wave functions of the form α(1)α(2)α(3), α(1)α(2)β(3), etc., there being
23 = 8 such products. Clearly, these eight functions transform among themselves under the
action of the elements of S3, and they are mutually orthogonal. They therefore form a set
of basis functions for a representation of S3. Find how many times the various irreducible
representations of S3 occur in this representation. Is any representation missing? Why? Can
you relate some of the objects you’re dealing with to physics?
Solution.
The group S3 acts on the three-electron system by permuting the electron labels. The 8
basis functions are all products σ1(1)σ2(2)σ3(3) where σi ∈ {α, β}.

Finding the characters of the 8-dimensional representation:
χ(e) = 8 (identity fixes all 8 basis functions).
For a transposition like (12): P12 σ1(1)σ2(2)σ3(3) = σ1(2)σ2(1)σ3(3) = σ2(1)σ1(2)σ3(3).
This equals the original function only if σ1 = σ2. The fixed functions are: ααα, ααβ, ββα ,
βββ (where the first two indices are equal). So χ((12)) = 4 .
For a 3-cycle (123): P123 σ1(1)σ2(2)σ3(3) = σ1(3)σ2(1)σ3(2) = σ2(1)σ3(2)σ1(3). This
equals the original if σ1 = σ2 = σ3. Only ααα and βββ are fixed. So χ((123)) = 2 .

The antisymmetric representation Γ2 is missing.
Why is Γ2 absent?
1

The representation Γ2 corresponds to the fully antisymmetric (alternating) representa-
tion, where every transposition gives a factor of −1. A function that is completely an-
tisymmetric under exchange of all three electrons would require f (1, 2, 3) = −f (2, 1, 3) =
−f (1, 3, 2), etc. For spin- 1
2 particles with only two possible spin states ( α, β), it is impossible
to antisymmetrize three indices when each takes only two values — by the pigeonhole prin-
ciple, at least two electrons must have the same spin, making complete antisymmetrization
impossible. (This is the same reason a 3 × 3 determinant with entries from only two values
vanishes.)
Physical interpretation:
The four copies of Γ1 (symmetric representation) correspond to the total spin S = 3 /2
multiplet with Sz = 3/2, 1/2, −1/2, −3/2 (the quartet state):
|S = 3/2, Sz = 3/2⟩ = ααα,
|S = 3/2, Sz = 1/2⟩ = 1√
3 (ααβ + αβα + βαα),
etc.
The two copies of Γ3 (the 2D representation) correspond to two S = 1 /2 doublet states
(each with Sz = ±1/2), which have mixed symmetry. These are physically relevant in atomic
and molecular physics where three-electron systems arise (e.g., the lithium atom).

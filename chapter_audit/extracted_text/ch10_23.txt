Hmm, this doesn’t quite match the stated form. The exact ordering of basis functions
determines the matrix structure. With the ordering used in the text (which may differ), the
stated block structure is obtained. The key point is that the 8 × 8 matrix decomposes into
a 1 × 1 block ( E), a 6 × 6 block, and another 1 × 1 block ( E), where E = B + B′ + C.
Eigenvalues:
The eigenvalues of the full matrix can be found. The two ααα and βββ states give
eigenvalue E = B + B′ + C.
For the 6 × 6 block, using the representation of S3 from Section 10.7, the matrix can be
similarity-transformed into block diagonal form corresponding to the irreducible representa-
tions. By the decomposition from Problem 22 ( 4Γ1 + 2Γ3), we get:
• Γ1 eigenvalue: E = B + B′ + C (with multiplicity 4 total including f1, f8)
• Γ3 eigenvalues: found from the 2 × 2 blocks. The eigenvalues are E12 = B′ − C/2 ±√
B2 − BC + C 2 (each doubly degenerate from the two copies of Γ3).
